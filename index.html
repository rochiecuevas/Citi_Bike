Citi Bike in New York City

Citi Bike is a bike rental program that allows each biker to undock a bike at a Citi Bike station, ride it for as long as his/her pass is valid, then redock the bike at any Citi Bike station in his/her destination. And it offers different pricing options, depending on how often one wants to use the Citi Bike. This page attempts to get some insights into how people in New York City used Citi Bike in 2018: Who uses Citi Bike? Where do people go when they use Citi Bike? What does Citi Bike station traffic flow look like on any given day in 2018?

The 2018 data is downloadable from Citi Bike's System Data website and is visualised here using Tableau Public.

Who used Citi Bike?

Citi Bike gathers demographic information from its users, such as age, subscriber type, and gender. The data for 2018 shows that there were more male than female Citi Bike users in all age groups. Moreover, the median trip durations for each age group indicates that female Citi Bike users typically took longer trips than male Citi Bike users. Perhaps, marketing strategies, bike specifications, and road safety features that could attract the female biking market needs to be enhanced to increase female use of Citi Bike.

Most of the bike users were in the 26–30 year age group, for both males and females. The minimum age for using Citi Bike is 16 years old; the market could not be expanded to younger bikers. The data shows, however, that there is still potential in

increasing Citi Bike penetration into the 16–20 year old market, particularly to the demographic that does not own bikes. Placing docks in close proximity to schools and promotions through influencers might help grow this particular demographic.

Majority (95%) of the users of Citi Bike were "subscribers", which means that they have bought annual memberships for the program. The "customers", or those who bought day-passes, probably used other bike rental services or had their own bikes; using Citi Bike was most likely a one-off for them. Customers could also include tourists who wanted to explore the Big Apple using pedal power. It, therefore, appears that the pricing structure has successfully attracted yearly subscriptions to the service.

Which Citi Bike stations needed more docks?

In 2018, Citi Bike docking stations experienced positive net traffic flows on most days (i.e., more bikes were returned to the stations than rented out). April 2018, notably, had two days of net traffic outflow. November 2018, on the other hand, had the three highest days of net traffic inflow. Click on a month or a day (a bar in the bar graph) to see which Citi Bike stations experienced net traffic inflows and outflows. Most of the stations in Jersey City, Queens, and Brooklyn experienced net traffic outflows. Manhattan, on the other hand, can be divided into two regions based on traffic flow: Uptown neighbourhoods mostly had net traffic outflows while midtown and downtown

neighbourhoods mostly had net influx of Citi Bike units. These results indicate that there might be a shortage of bike docks in midtown and downtown Manhattan while stations outside these neighbourhoods probably had seen a shortage of bikes. To address these potential issues on bike supply, Citi Bike may need to install more docks in the high-influx stations in Manhattan and improve the replenishment of bike supplies in the other boroughs covered by the program.
