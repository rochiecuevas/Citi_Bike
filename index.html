Citi Bike in New York City

Citi Bike is a bike rental program that allows each biker to undock a bike at a Citi Bike station, ride it for as long as his/her pass is valid, then redock the bike at any Citi Bike station in his/her destination. And it offers different pricing options, depending on how often one wants to use the Citi Bike. This page attempts to get some insights into how people in New York City used Citi Bike in 2018: Who uses Citi Bike? Where do people go when they use Citi Bike? What does Citi Bike station traffic flow look like on any given day in 2018?

The 2018 data is downloadable from Citi Bike's System Data website and is visualised here using Tableau Public.

Data notes:

Age. The minimum age for using the Citi Bike in New York City is 16 years. Hence, all ages below 16 are excluded from the analysis. For older people, data on bike use by people 60–79 years old is growing. However, 2009 statistics on bike use by people in their 80s indicate that they contribute far less than the other age groups to bike ridership, both in terms of number of trips and distance travelled. Hence, rides whose calculated ages are above 80 years are excluded from the analysis.

Gender. The Citi Bike data classifies rider gender as male, female, and unknown. To understand the differences in Citi Bike use between males and females, the trips whose riders have no gender information were excluded from the analysis.

Trip duration. Trips exceeding 180 mins were excluded from the analysis because: (1) it is unlikely that Citi Bike members would like to incur additional charges if they go well beyond their single or annual pass trip time allowances; (2) excessively long trips may be indicators of anomalies such as lost bikes or improper docking of bikes in stations.

Trips shorter then 90 seconds were also excluded because these could possibly be registered by bikers redocking their bikes to ensure that the bikes were secure. Defective bikes, as implied by round trips to the same station within five minutes, are removed from the analysis as well.
